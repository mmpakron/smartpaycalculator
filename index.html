How it works

The simple math behind each calculator.

Hourly → Salary

Annual = Hourly × Hours per Week × Weeks per Year Monthly = Annual ÷ 12 Weekly = Annual ÷ Weeks per Year

Salary → Hourly

Hourly = Annual Salary ÷ (Hours per Week × Weeks per Year)

Overtime Pay

Regular Pay = Hourly × Regular Hours (this pay period) Overtime Pay = Hourly × Overtime Hours × Multiplier Total Pay = Regular Pay + Overtime Pay Annualized = Total Pay × Pay Periods per Year

These calculators show gross estimates (before taxes/deductions) and are meant for quick comparisons.

FAQ

Common questions people ask.

Does this include taxes or deductions?

No. All results are gross estimates (before taxes, deductions, retirement, insurance, etc.).

Why does monthly pay differ from biweekly or semi-monthly pay?

Monthly is annual ÷ 12. Biweekly and semi-monthly have different pay period counts (26 vs 24), so the per-check amount differs.

How should I use the overtime calculator?

Select your pay period, then enter regular hours and overtime hours for that pay period. Adjust the multiplier if your employer pays different rates.

Is the annualized overtime estimate accurate?

It’s a simple estimate: (this pay period total) × (pay periods per year). If overtime varies, your annual total will vary.
